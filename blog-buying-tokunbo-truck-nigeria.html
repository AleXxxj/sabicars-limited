A Complete Guide to Buying a Tokunbo Truck in Nigeria

Sabicars Editorial · November 18, 2024 · 8 min read

Everything you need to know before buying a used truck — inspection checklist, documentation, and what to avoid.

Buying a truck in Nigeria — whether a tipper, container truck, tanker, or flatbed — is a significant investment. Get it wrong and you'll be spending more on repairs than you earn in operations. Get it right and you have an asset that generates income for years. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Types of Trucks Available in Nigeria

  • Tipper trucks — for construction, sand, gravel, and earth-moving operations
  • Container trucks (prime movers) — for hauling 20ft or 40ft containers at seaports and warehouses
  • Tanker trucks — for fuel, water, or liquid cargo transportation
  • Flatbed trucks — for oversized loads, machinery, and construction equipment
  • Refrigerated trucks — for food and pharmaceutical cold chain logistics

Key Inspection Points Before You Buy

Never buy a truck without physically inspecting it — or having a trusted mechanic inspect it. Here's what to check:

  1. Engine condition — Start the engine cold. Listen for knocking sounds, excessive smoke (blue or black), or rough idle. A clean engine start is a good sign.
  2. Transmission and gearbox — Test all gears while driving. Trucks with manual transmission should shift smoothly without grinding.
  3. Frame and chassis — Look underneath for cracks, welds, or signs of serious accident damage that was repaired.
  4. Tyres and axles — Check tyre wear patterns. Uneven wear can indicate alignment or suspension issues.
  5. Hydraulics (for tippers) — Test the tipping mechanism under load if possible. Check for oil leaks around hydraulic cylinders.
  6. Brakes — Air brake trucks should hold pressure. Test emergency braking function.

Documentation You Must Verify

This is where many Nigerians get burned. Always verify:

  • Vehicle registration (Customs papers for imported trucks)
  • Proof of ownership — must match the seller's identity
  • Chassis number on the body matches the papers
  • Engine number matches the papers
  • No encumbrance (the truck is not under loan/lien to a bank)
  • Road worthiness certificate (for operational trucks)
Warning

Be very cautious of sellers who cannot produce original customs papers for imported trucks, or who are in a rush to sell without proper documentation. Buy only from verified dealers like Sabicars where documentation is guaranteed.

Popular Truck Brands in Nigeria

  • Mack — American-made, extremely durable, widely serviced across Nigeria
  • Volvo — Swedish engineering, fuel-efficient, good for long-haul
  • Man Diesel — Popular for tippers, German reliability
  • Scania — High-performance, excellent for heavy loads
  • Sino Truck (HOWO) — Chinese-made, budget-friendly but requires careful sourcing
