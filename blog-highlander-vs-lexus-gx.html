Toyota Highlander vs Lexus GX 460: Which Should You Buy?

Sabicars Editorial · December 5, 2024 · 6 min read

Both are reliable, both are premium. We break down the key differences to help you choose right.

Two of the most popular vehicles at Sabicars — the Toyota Highlander and the Lexus GX 460 — are also two of the most compared. Both come from the Toyota family, both are reliable, and both are well-suited for Nigerian roads. But they serve different needs and different buyers. Let's break it down.

Platform & Build

The Toyota Highlander is built on a unibody (car-like) platform. This gives it a smoother, more car-like ride, better fuel economy, and a more refined experience on tarmac roads. However, it has less ground clearance and off-road capability than a body-on-frame SUV.

The Lexus GX 460 shares its platform with the Toyota Prado — a proper body-on-frame truck chassis. This makes it significantly more capable on rough terrain, with better ground clearance, a locking center differential, and genuine off-road credentials. If your vehicle will regularly encounter bad roads, the GX wins.

Engine & Performance

  • Highlander: 3.5L V6, ~295 hp — smooth, refined, excellent for city driving
  • GX 460: 4.6L V8, ~301 hp — more power, more torque, better for load and terrain

For highway and city use, the Highlander's V6 is more fuel-efficient. For rough roads, heavy loads, or frequent off-road use, the GX's V8 torque is a clear advantage.

Interior & Luxury

Being a Lexus, the GX 460 has a slight edge in interior luxury — premium leather, wood trim, and a refined cabin that feels more upmarket. The Highlander's interior is excellent for Toyota standards but doesn't quite match the GX's Lexus refinement.

On passenger capacity: the Highlander seats 7–8 comfortably across three rows. The GX seats 7 but the third row is tighter — better for kids than adults.

Fuel Economy

In real Nigerian driving conditions:

  • Highlander V6: roughly 9–12 litres per 100km
  • GX 460 V8: roughly 13–16 litres per 100km

If fuel costs are a significant concern, the Highlander is the more economical choice.

Price & Value

In the Nigerian Tokunbo market, the GX 460 typically commands a higher price than the Highlander for equivalent model years — partly due to its Lexus badge and V8 engine, partly due to lower supply volume.

The Bottom Line

Buy the Highlander if: you prioritise fuel economy, passenger space, and smooth on-road performance.

Buy the GX 460 if: you encounter rough roads regularly, want stronger off-road capability, or prefer the Lexus brand and V8 power.
